The documentation bottleneck

DIY assistive technology manuals are trapped in fragmented PDFs and Word files — inaccessible, outdated, and unavailable in most languages. Rural families, global makers, and clinicians lose hours searching for instructions that often don't exist in their language or bandwidth constraints.

PDF silos

Manuals live across random Google Drives, university pages, and forums. No version control, no discoverability.

Language walls

95% of manuals are English-only. Families in Peru, Kenya, or rural US can't adapt toys without costly translation.

High bandwidth, low access

PDFs are large; downloading manuals excludes communities with unreliable Wi-Fi. Our system delivers lightweight, text-first formats.

HuskyOpen Infrastructure

A headless, schema-driven pipeline that turns crowd-sourced adaptation knowledge into accessible, multilingual manuals — automatically.

Standardized Schema

Extract, Transform, Load framework. Every manual is structured once — parts, steps, warnings, images — and can be re-rendered for any audience.

AI-Powered Translation

Native i18n support. Write in English, instantly generate manuals in Spanish, Swahili, Arabic, or any language. Low-cost, high-accuracy.

Multi-format Outputs

Beginner style guide, expert cheat sheet, screen-reader optimized HTML, Braille-ready, and lightweight offline versions.

Content as a Service (CaaS) for AT

One source of truth powers everything: dynamic web manuals, printable PDFs, and API access for organizations like Makers Making Change, GBG, and global clinics. No more outdated attachments — just live, versioned, accessible documentation.
